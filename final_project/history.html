Introduction

The Corvette C5, produced from 1997 to 2004, was a significant leap forward for the Corvette brand. With a completely new design and improved performance, it marked a new era for American sports cars.

Design Innovations

The C5 introduced a new design language for the Corvette, emphasizing aerodynamics and modern styling. Features like the fixed roof coupe and convertible models showcased its versatility.

Performance Upgrades

Equipped with a powerful LS1 engine, the C5 offered outstanding performance. The introduction of the Magnetic Selective Ride Control further enhanced its handling capabilities.

A Legacy of Racing

The C5 was not only a road car but also made its mark in motorsports, with numerous victories in races such as the 24 Hours of Le Mans and various American Le Mans Series events.
